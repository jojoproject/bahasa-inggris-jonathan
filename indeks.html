Welcome to the relative pronoun learning session

Definition

Relative clauses are also sometimes referred to as adjective clauses, because they identify or give us additional information about the subject of the independent clause they relate to. Like adjective, these clauses in some way describe that subject.

Relative pronouns, like conjunctions, are words that join clauses-in this case, a relative clause to its main clause. The type of relative pronoun used depends on what kind of noun is being described.

Discussion

Function

Who

  • "Who" is used to refer to a person and is the subject of a clause.
  • For example: "The person who won the award is a well-known scientist."

Whom

  • "Whom" is used to refer to a person and functions as the object of a clause
  • For example: "The winner of the award, whom I admire, is a well-known scientist."

Whose

  • "Whose" is used to indicate possession and can refer to both people and objects.
  • For example: "The scientist whose research changed the world is the winner of the award."

Which

  • "Which" is used to refer to objects and is the subject or object of a clause.
  • For example: “The book which I am reading is fascinating.”

That

  • Preview the text"That" is used to refer to people, objects, or ideas and can be the subject or object of a clause. It is less formal than "Who", "Whom", and "Whose".
  • For example: “The award that was given to the scientist was well-deserved.”

    1. The shirt __________ I bought is too big.
    1. Who
    2. Whom
    3. Which
    4. That
    2. The singer, __________ voice is incredible, is performing tonight.
    1. Who
    2. Whom
    3. Which
    4. That
    3. The friend __________ you met yesterday is coming over today.
    1. Who
    2. Whom
    3. Which
    4. That
    4. The teacher __________ taught the class was very knowledgeable.
    1. Who
    2. Whom
    3. Which
    4. That
    5. The book ___ I read last night was very interesting.
    1. Who
    2. Whom
    3. Which
    4. That
